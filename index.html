V. РЕГУЛЯТОРНАЯ СРЕДА (EUDR, RED III)

EUDR после пакета упрощения (май 2026) устанавливает дедлайны: 30.12.2026 для крупных/средних операторов и 30.06.2027 для МСП. Требуется геолокация каждого участка и доказательство «deforestation‑free» по цепочке поставок (включая соевые и palm‑деривативы). [file:66][web:25]

RED III ужесточает критерии устойчивости для био‑топлива; новые лимиты по MOAH/MOSH в пищевых маслах и жирах требуют более жёсткого контроля вспомогательных веществ и упаковки. [file:66]

III. EUROPEAN REGIONAL MARKETS

Western & Northern Europe

Germany: key HVO consumer of vegetable oils; strong focus on UCO and technical fats. Oilseed acreage is stable; food sector shifts toward cold‑pressed and organic oils. [file:66]

Netherlands: Europe’s main logistics and processing hub; importers adapt contracts for EUDR and raise requirements for traceability and non‑GMO. [file:66][web:25]

United Kingdom: rapeseed acreage recovering by 14–15% y/y; RTFO caps food‑grade oils in biofuels, redirecting rapeseed oil back to food and feed. [file:66]

France, Belgium, Ireland emphasise local supply chain consolidation and quality management in logistics clusters (e.g., Antwerp) to mitigate logistics risk. [file:66]

Southern, Central & Eastern Europe

Italy: accelerating transition to waste‑based feedstocks in line with RED III; reliance on food‑grade palm oil in biofuels is declining but remains in specific niches. [file:66]

Poland: early‑June rainfall improved winter rapeseed conditions after May drought; outlook is stabilising, yet cost inflation remains a concern. [file:66]

Czech Republic: recent decree amendments tighten classification and labelling rules for spreadable fats and mixed products; retailers increasingly segregate plant‑based spreads from butter at shelf level. [file:66]

Estonia & Greece: small domestic processing base, high dependence on regional imports and harmonised logistics; Greece explores biofuel‑based marine bunkering and supports local sunflower cultivation. [file:66]

V. REGULATORY OUTLOOK (EUDR, RED III)

EUDR, following the May 2026 simplification package, sets compliance deadlines of 30 Dec 2026 for large/medium operators and 30 Jun 2027 for SMEs; geolocation and “deforestation‑free” proof are required for each relevant plot and supply chain segment. [file:66][web:25]

RED III tightens sustainability criteria for biofuels; new MOAH/MOSH limits in food oils and fats trigger stricter control of processing aids and packaging. [file:66]
